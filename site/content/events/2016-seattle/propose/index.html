Our call for proposals is currently open and will close at midnight on Feb 28th!

The final selections will be announced March 20th.

There will be three types of talks: 30 min, 5 min, and Open Space. Only the first two types require proposals.

  1. (8) 30 minute talks - On the main stage to the estimated 400 person audience.
  2. (10) 5 minute Ignite talks - On the main stage to the full audience with slides changing every 15 seconds (20 slides total). See Ignite sessions
  3. (30) Open Space talks - Occurring in breakout rooms and spaces. These will be smaller group discussions, welcome even without a prepared presentation. See Open Space explanation

How to submit a proposal: Send an email to proposals-seattle-2016@devopsdays.org with the following information:

  1. Your working title (can be changed later)
  2. The type of your talk (30 min or Ignite) (also a good place to indicate if you'd do either)
  3. A description or abstract
  4. Media links to this talk or a prior talk. (To help us see that you and the topic can engage the large audience)

Our top criteria for the final selections will be:

  1. New content: content not yet presented at other conferences, at this conference, or a new angle to an existing problem
  2. New presenters: people who are new to the space and have insightful stuff to say; we want to hear everybody's voice
  3. No vendor pitches: as much as we value vendors and sponsors, we just don't think this is the right forum. You can demo at your table or during Open Space.

Additional Criteria:

  1. Be specific (about 20 lines)
  2. Detail is good... but not as important as explaining why your proposal would be interesting
  3. Propose your own talk; please don't have your marketing department do it for you.
  4. Multiple proposals are welcome!
  5. Nominations are welcome! if you know someone who has content/experience relevant to the DevOps conversation, please point us in their direction!

Thank you for helping make DevOpsDays Seattle at great event!
